A brief outline of my research skills (aka "what I can do"):
- **molecular cloning**
- **expression of recombinant proteins** in all major expression systems (bacterial, yeast, insect, mammalian cells), including:
  - introduction of genetic material into yeast cells by electroporation, clone selection
  - preparation of recombinant baculoviruses, growing of insect cell lines, insect cell transfection, viral amplification
  - growing of mammalian cells, cell transfection
- **protein purification** (FPLC, HPLC)
- **biochemical & biophysical protein characterization**:
  - electrophoretic methods
  - *in vitro* chemical modification of proteins
  - circular dichroism (CD) spetroscopy
  - isothermal titration calorimetry (ITC)
  - microscale thermophoresis (MST) ans surface plasmon resonance (SPR)
  - light scattering (LS) to characterize macromolecules and complexes in solution
- **structural biology**:
  - X-ray crystallography - all structure determination steps, i.e. from crystallization through diffraction data collection to phasing, model building and refinement
  - small angle X-ray scattering (SAXS) - all steps from sample preparation to data analysis and interpretation (*ab initio* and model-supported modeling, ...)
  - molecular dynamics (MD) simulation of biological systems (all-atom and coarse-grained MD simulations) - all steps from system preparation to simulation, analysis and interpretation
  - modeling of proteins and nucleic acids, both *ab initio* as well as template-based and restraints-guided modeling, including (macro)molecular docking
  - advanced molecular graphics in UCSF Chimera and PyMOL
- **(bio)informatics**:
  - proficiency in all major operating systems (Linux and other UNIX-like operating systems, macOS, Microsoft Windows)
  - programming (Python, Bash)
  - all general bioinformatics skills: databases, sequence alignments and phylogenetic tree construction, etc.
  - graph theory
